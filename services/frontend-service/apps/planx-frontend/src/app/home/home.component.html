Usable, Interoperable and Reusable Planning Functionalities

Usability

Usability is ensured according to the following criteria:

Effectiveness Efficiency User satisfaction

Interoperability

Interoperability is thus composed of the following factors:

Organizational interoperability Semantic interoperability Syntactic interoperability Connectivity interoperability

Reusability

Reusability is composed of four quality characteristics:

Portability Flexibility Understandability Confidence

Featured Functionality Classes:

Core Functionalities

Leonard-Barton, Prahalad and Hamel defined core functionalities as functionalities which satisfy the following three criteria:

  • Does the competence provide access to a wide variety of markets?
  • Does the competence make a significant contribution to the perceived customer benefits of the end products?
  • Is the competence challenging to imitate?

Applied to the context of AI planning, the question to be answered is the significant contribution to the perceived customer benefit.

Enabling Functionalities

Enabling functionalities are defined as functionalities that do not provide a competitive advantage on their own but are necessary for other functionalities.

Supplemental Functionalities

Supplemental functionalities are easily accessible and provide a competitive advantage for the user. This class of functionalities often requires enabling functionalities to run.

Endpoint Functionalities

Endpoint Functionalities are sink or source nodes from a technical point of view at the system level.

Transforming Functionalities

Transforming functionalities convert change one datastructures to another. Thereby a defined behavior is taken into account.

System-Managing Functionalities

System management functionalities consume data flow and respond to given conditions.

Routing Functionalities

The routing function helps to route messages through the system.
